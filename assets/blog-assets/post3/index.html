My vocal sound seems me funny and disgusting

Author: Atul Rajput | Date: 10th Jan, 2024

It's not uncommon for people to feel self-conscious or uncomfortable about the sound of their own voice. Many factors can contribute to this perception, including personal preferences, cultural influences, or even sensitivity to certain tones. However, it's essential to recognize that your perception of your own voice may not align with how others perceive it.

Here are a few things to consider:

Self-perception bias:
The way you hear your voice when you speak is different from how others hear it. This is because when you speak, you hear a combination of sound conducted through the air and vibrations conducted through your skull. When you hear a recording of your voice, it may sound unfamiliar and possibly uncomfortable because it lacks the vibrations you're used to.

Acceptance:
Everyone has unique qualities to their voice. Instead of focusing on perceived flaws, try to appreciate the individuality and uniqueness of your voice. Accepting your voice can boost your confidence and make you feel more comfortable when speaking.

Practice and familiarity:
The more you hear your recorded voice, the more accustomed you may become to its sound. Regular practice and exposure can help reduce discomfort and make you more comfortable with your own voice.

Seek feedback:
Ask for constructive feedback from friends or trusted individuals. They may provide a more objective perspective and offer positive insights that can help you appreciate your voice.

Professional help:
If your discomfort with your voice is affecting your self-esteem or well-being, consider seeking guidance from a speech therapist or vocal coach. They can provide personalized advice and exercises to help you feel more confident in your vocal abilities.

Remember that everyone has unique characteristics in their voice, and what might sound funny or strange to you may be perfectly normal and acceptable to others. Embracing and appreciating your voice is an important step towards building self-confidence.
